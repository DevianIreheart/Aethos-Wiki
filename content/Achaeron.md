**Achaeron**, the Deathless King, is god of the undead and patron of necromancers, unscrupulous mages, and those seeking eternal life.

---

>![[dmgcover.jpg|288x288]]
>- **Alignment.** Chaos
>- **Suggested Domains.** Death, Grave
>- **Colours.** Black, mould, green
>- **Symbol.** Skeletal Hand

## Description
Achaeron grants his favoured supplicants life beyond death by gradually infusing their souls with dark energies until the corruption prevents [[Osira]] from taking them to the afterlife. So violating of the natural order is this process that most regular folk and all animals have a visceral, gut-retching response when in proximity of one so affected.

**Worship.** The cost of unending life is steep. Adhering to Achaeron's heinous rejection of nature demands the following:
- Pursuing mastery of necromancy and the magics of death
- Engage in regular bloodletting, of both oneself and others
- Monthly ritual sacrifice at a shrine of Achaeron

**Temples & Shrines.** Temples to Achaeron are few in number and are usually isolated away from large towns and cities. Along with the smaller shrines dedicated to him, they are forlorn places seeping with corruption that saps the vitality from their surroundings. Statues of Achaeron show him standing within a seven pointed star. Folk lay the corpses of sacrificed animals and people about his feet. At large shrines, the blood never seems to dry.

**Depiction.** Achaeron is typically shown as a tall, skeletal man with the head of a black jackal. He holds in one hand his staff, capped with a skull with flaming eyes, and on his belt is a curved sword named Kopesh. Together his sword and staff represent his control over death and undeath, the two aspects of necromantic magics.

**Holy Day.** The Day of The Dead, Desenacris, falls on the 7th of Soulsgard. Within the human realms of Aethos it is a day and night which are considered cursed. People only exit their homes during the day if they need food or essential supplies, but otherwise remain locked within their homes for fear of the wandering dead, as the tradition goes, which stalk the shadows in search of new souls to add to their undead legions. For the faithful of Achaeron, the night of Desenacris represents a time of recognition for their devotion and work throughout the past year. Sacrifices are made, devotions are offered, and perhaps, should The Unborn deem them worthy, boons of necromantic power and knowledge may be bestowed upon a select few disciples.

**Commandments of Achaeron**
•     Fight against the living and the Dawn Realms that they may join the endless legions of the dead
•     Your life should be spent in service to death – kill, sacrifice, and destroy and you shall be granted an eternal undeath
