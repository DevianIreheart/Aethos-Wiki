## A land of cosmic mysticism

[[Aethos]] (a continent in the world of [[Etharia]]) is a medieval high fantasy setting full of monsters, magic, and feudal powers. Its towns and cities are disparate points of safety within vast untamed wildernesses teeming with deadly beasts, forgotten secrets, and hidden treasures. In Aethos, humans and their counterparts are part of a dangerous ecosystem where they are not the masters. Fear, caution, and superstition dictate how daily life plays out, and the deadly wilds are accepted as something to coexist with and adapt to, not as a problem that could feasibly ever be solved. The year is currently **1504 of the Modern Age (M.A.)**.

## Animistic Deities
All objects, places, and creatures are presided over by distinct spiritual essences capable of helping or harming mortal interests. When the dominion is localised, such as to a particular weapon, forge, home, river or a mountain, then its essence is referred to as a [[spirit]]. If the dominion is more ubiquitous, such as [[Vulkan|fire]], [[Ela|the sea]], or [[Zerendar|storms]], then its essence is typically referred to as a [[god]]. 

[[Gods]] and [[Spirits]] preside over the physical world, not the metaphysical or conceptual. For example: there is a spirit of a sword, a [[Sanguis|god of blood]], and multiple deities presiding over each aspect of the [[Gods of Death|the dead]], but there is no god of war or love - but certain deities may naturally be associated with these concepts. 

Spirits exist in all things, but only in things that are very old does their being have any recognisable consciousness, power or sentience. Thus, the oldest things in the world have the most powerful and active spirits, and are therefore deserving of great reverence and respect, lest their spirits be angered. 

Before the [[Modern Age]], the most powerful spirits – the [[Gods]] – could take physical forms and influence the world directly. Yet this ability was taken from them in an apocalyptic event called the [[Severance]]. Since then, the gods have ceased to interact with the world physically. Monuments, statues and artistic impressions were erected in their honour, and eventually superstition and dogma grew robust enough that folk came to worship them as intangible spirits and gods, rather than as the corporeal beings they once were.

## Magic
In Aethos basic magic is somewhat commonplace, though mages are elitist and consider their craft a secret to be kept closely guarded, making true masters a rare sight indeed. Young mages learn about magic by apprenticing to one of the continent's [[One Hundred Masters]] – a very simple system which ensures the secrets of magic are only given to whomever a master mage deems worthy. Without an apprenticeship, even the most gifted sorcerer can only get so far on their own. Only the [[College of Arkyne]] provides any sort of institutionalised tutelage, though its pupil numbers are never above a dozen at any time, and each student remains apprenticed to a master whilst in attendance. 


## Astrological Motifs
The power of magic is linked to the [[The Weave|Cosmic Weave]]. Astrology, astronomy, and astrophysics are core motivic elements. [[Astromancy]] is a revered type of cosmic-themed magic, and those few who can use it are called **Astromancers**. Much of Aethorian religion, culture, politics and magic is influenced heavily by the movements of the cosmos, be it the patterns of the stars or the tracking of celestial bodies across the sky throughout the year. 


## Cataclysm: The Severance
[[The Severance]] was an apocalyptic level event that brought an end to the enlightened age of gods (before the year 0 of the current calendar). It saw the sudden disappearance of the gods and the anarchic collapse of all the world’s civilisations.

The world split into hemispheres: the **[[Dusk Realms]]** (eastern) were completely ruined by that apocalypse and are now solely the domain of evil realms, monsters, and chaotic magics. The **[[Dawn Realms]]** (western) were not so overcome by chaos, and civilisation managed to start anew. The **[[Kingdom of Mercy]]** resides at the heart of the Dawn Realms and is amongst its most powerful nations.

## The Long Dark - Solar Eclipse
The beginning and end of each [[Calendar & Time|year]] is marked by a 20-day total eclipse of the sun by [[Sanguis]], the larger of the two moons (that Sanguis is really a planet is unknown to most folk). Occurring reliably at midwinter, this event plunges the whole world into a deep cooling phase, heightening the rigors of winter to the extreme and severely straining the resources and logistics of every settlement. Every facet of the world's ecosystem has adapted in some way to endure or thrive throughout this period. Some predatory monsters become very active, whilst others, along with most flora, go into deep hibernation. Surface settlements adopt strict rationing of fuel for heating, and the services of mages and adventurers reach peak demand as they struggle to provide folk relief from natural disasters and monster attacks. 

## History & Timeline
### The Conjuring 
**The Conjuring** is the name given to the mythic history of the world, [[Etheris]], beginning with its creation, and covering all the ages which passed up until current, Modern Age. Most folk know a basic version of this story and can reference its defining events, factions, and personalities. 

**The Time of Chaos** is the first era of the Conjuring and preceded the gods’. It was a period of elemental chaos which shaped the land and seas of the world and gave rise to its first wild creatures and uncivilised peoples. 

**The Age of Gods**, also called The Enlightenment, was a period that saw the appearance of deities and their establishment as masters over magic and the four elements. They shaped the world as they desired, though rarely did they see eye to eye, or work towards the same ends. Some gods built and ruled mortal empires, some sought to uncover the secrets of magic, some revelled in inflicting pain and suffering. For mortals, the world by the end of the Enlightenment was one of magically and technologically advanced civilisations ruled by gods locked in a stalemate of unending, often bloody, conflicts and machinations in attempts to gain an upper hand.

### The Severance
This final chapter of The Conjuring began with a cataclysm of unknown origin and nature which expunged the gods from the world, erasing them along with the understanding of their advanced magic and technology. The Severance led to a rapid, anarchic devolution of civilisation. Without the gods as leaders, society collapsed, wars ravaged world, and wilderness and monsters reclaimed all but the most resolute of cities. 

Half the world – the half now called "Dusk" to which none dare venture – was completely laid to ruin. The other hemisphere, "Dawn," suffered immeasurably too, but in those lands forces of order eventually regained an upper hand over the chaos. Civilisation, battered and beleaguered, continued on. Knowledge of all but the most basic magic, science and enlightened wisdom from the Age of Gods was lost to the Severance’s centuries of anarchy. Only scraps and ruins of that time remain – scraps now fought over by adventurers, lords, kingdoms and empires.

### The Modern Age
The fifteen centuries of the Modern Age have seen an enduring struggle of order against ruin. Common folk eke out a living whilst challenged by monsters, banditry, and wars. Scholars and mages work to uncover the secrets of the enlightenment, occasionally regaining scraps of powerful knowledge, but not all of it ends up in the right hands or being used for the common good. All the while, nobles play at their eternal game of politics to secure any advantage for their realm and people.

## History of Mercy

### Foundations c.0 – 500 M.A.
After the [[Severance]], three new tribal realms were established in the bountiful [[Mercian Vale]]. Each realm claimed a ruined city of the [[Age of Enlightenment|Enlightenment]] as its capital, rebuilding them into their seats of power. During this time the first dwarves arrived on [[Aethos]] from their war-ravaged homeland of [[Dusk Realms|Dusk]]. Some clans integrated into Mercian cities, some established their own holdings, while a few clans remained as wandering, self- sufficient nomadic groups. 

Protected by a wall of mountains on land, yet open to the plentiful [[Serpent Sea]], the peoples of the Mercian Vale - the [[Tourelle|Tourellans]], [[Navarre|Navarrene]] and the Fells - prospered for centuries, but were ever in contention for supremacy over each other.

### Elven Shaedhe Dynasty 531 – 878 M.A.
In 531 M.A. the lands that would become the [[Kingdom of Mercy|Mercian realms]], distracted by wars between themselves, were conquered swiftly by an [[Elf|Elven]] empire called the [[Shaedhe Dynasty]]. 

The [[Mercian|Mercians]] found the dynasty to be benevolent rulers however, and they gained much in the ways of the arcane, civil engineering, agriculture, the arts and many other disciplines and industries. In return for these gifts of knowledge, large numbers of elven immigrants were allowed to settle throughout the [[Mercian Vale]]. With them they brought their own religions and gods, which over time were adopted into the Mercian pantheon. Soon enough, [[Elf|elves]] and [[half-elves]] came to make up a large portion of the region’s population.

Guided by [[Elf|elven]] principles and empowered by their understanding of magic, the [[Kingdom of Mercy|Mercian realms]] worked together and prospered. They traded goods instead of sword blows, and all reaped the reward.

### The Incidian Scourge c.901 – 1041 M.A.
In 901 M.A., the tyrannical human empire of [[Incidius]] sent its crusaders across the seas to “liberate” the continent of [[Aethos]] from its elven ways. Empowered by stolen [[gnomish]] black- powder weapons of [[Cannonar]], but facing off against [[Mercians]] trained in [[Elf|elven]] magic, the Empire’s legionnaires bled dearly for every inch of [[Mercian Vale|The Vale]] they took. From 901 to 908 M.A. [[Incidius]] fought a costly war to conquer the [[Mercian Vale]], and afterwards, their appetite for extending the crusade into Aethos’ southern realms was lost. The Mercians had paid dearly and were subjugated into a hateful empire, but they had ensured the freedom of the southern realms. Thus the war of [[Kingdom of Mercy|Mercy]] and [[Incidius]] is known today as the ‘[[Mercian War of Sacrifice]].’

The [[Empire of Incidius|Incidian]] iron fist of ruin and reform then ruled for a century. Their armies burned and scoured the land of any elven influence they found; statues, temples, homes, artwork, libraries, palaces – all were torn down, defaced, and destroyed. In 910 M.A., their [[Edict of Unification]] brought the [[Tourelle]], [[Navarri]] and [[Fellfolk]] under a single ruler and name: The [[Province of Mercy]].

### Kingdom Established 1041.03.01 M.A.
The decline of the Incidian Empire throughout the early 11th century saw, on [[Florius]] 1st 1041 M.A., the [[Province of Mercy]] declare its independence and become the [[Kingdom of Mercy]]. On that first day of spring, forever more to be known as Mercy’s [[Day of Sovereignty]], the borders of the three old realms became borders of [[grand duchies of Mercy|Grand Duchies]], and the [[Mercian Vale]] crowned its first Queen, [[Alina Valencia I]], to rule over it from the capital city of [[Revere]]. 

### Orthane’s Betrayal 1056-1059 M.A.
In 1056, the archmage [[Orthane]] of Mercy’s [[Mercian Council of Mages|Council of Mages]] attempted to assassinate the ailing [[Queen Alina I]], but escaped immediate capture and went on to lead an army of undead in conquest against the kingdom. Ultimately the [[Zephyr Knights]] and their dragons defeated [[Orthane]], but his betrayal had undermined the people’s trust in mages forever. 

Alina dissolved her council of mages and implemented the [[Concord of Arkyne]] – an agreement which established terms of alliance between [[Kingdom of Mercy|Mercy]] and [[College of Arkyne|Arkyne]] whilst simultaneously decreeing that no mages in the kingdom could hold noble rank or own land. With that, magic and politics were separated, and the ability for mages to make a living without subservience to the nobility was severely curtailed. 

### Mercian Dominance
The rule of the [[Shaedhe Dynasty]] and then [[Empire of Incidius|Incidius]] were tumultuous and destructive at times. But the Mercians learned from their overlords such that, upon gaining their independence, they could apply the good and do away with the bad. 

Cultivating close ties to the [[Mages of Arkyne]], together with the statecraft learned of the [[Shaedhe Dynasty|Shaedhe]] and the industrial might of [[Empire of Incidius|Incidius]], the last few centuries have seen the Kingdom of Mercy build itself into one of two dominant powers on [[Aethos]] militarily, economically, and technologically – matched only by the [[Empire of Phairos]]. The Mercian capital, [[Revere]], is the largest city in the world, and just a few miles from it across the [[Firebrand Strait]] lies the islands of [[Exandros]] and the [[College of Arkyne]]. In [[Navarre]], the city of Anchorcliff stands as a global bastion of science and engineering, home to many of the greatest minds the modern age has to offer, and thanks to the brave soldiers of [[Fellhold]], the borders of the realm have been successfully defended time and again from enemy invasion. 

## Rise of Phairos
*Bronze Age Egyptian Influence. (Fair-ross)*
*People: The Phairosi*

The last century (1400-1500 M.A.) has seen the rise of the [[Empire of Phairos|Phairosi Empire]] and their expansion well beyond the [[Sahran desert]] of central [[Aethos]]. This theocratic empire is ruled by a [[Conclave of Phairos|mage conclave]] whose leaders are worshipped as prophets of the sun god, [[Helos]]. [[Empire of Phairos|Phairos]] sees itself as enlightened liberators bringing peace and sophistication to the lesser nations it conquers. In many respects, this is perhaps not untrue: when the [[Empire of Phairos|Phairosi]] annex a new realm, they make a point of maintaining and celebrating that realms unique cultural identity. Because of this stance on cultural diversity, they have become a forward-thinking, robust society behind their battle- lines. 

Whilst there is no racial or religious segregation within their Empire, the [[Empire of Phairos|Phairosi]] do hold mages and spellcasters who aren’t in the [[Conclave of Phairos]] on very short leashes. In this they are utterly draconian. [[Unsanctioned mages]] are forbidden from travelling around the empire unsupervised. Only with the express permission of the conclave can any wizard, sorcerer or even a cleric live or work within its borders, and all must adhere to certain [[Phairosi Laws of Magic|restrictions]] on their magic as well.

## Religion
The [[Aethorian Orthodoxy|Aethorian Pantheon]] (Aethorian Orthodoxy) is an animistic religion: every river, mountain, tree, beast, or crafted object bears a spirit, while the most universal forces are revered as gods. Spirits are localised, gods are more universal. Before the [[Severance]], deities could manifest and walk the world, but now they endure only as incorporeal presences with their manifestations limited to imparting visions, causing mystical or natural events, etc. 

Spirits interact frequently with mortals and their manifestations are more direct and recognisable (a vision or dream, the toll of a bell, hallucinations of voices), while gods are distant and their manifestations are very subtle (a gentle breeze, a slight drop of temperature, the fleeting scent of roses, etc) and arguably not existent at all. 

Worship is polytheistic and situational, though rare monotheists dedicate themselves wholly to worship of a single deity. Regional preference shapes devotion—Calderon is favoured in Tourelle, Fir in Fellhold, Thuridin among duarvi—while Osira, Fayndal, and Sanguis embody death, vittles, and blood, and so their shrines are found most everywhere.
