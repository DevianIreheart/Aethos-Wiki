| [[Aethos Setting Overview]] |

**Aethorian Orthodoxy** - the primary faith of the Aethorian peoples - hinges on belief in and worship of a vast cast of animistic deities, collectively styled "**The Aethorian Pantheon.**" The peoples of [[Aethos]] typically worship the gods and spirits together as a group (_the Orthodox_, polytheists), though a rare few dedicate themselves wholly to a single deity (_Devotionists_, monotheists).

All deities are animistic (cf. [[shinto]]): they preside over tangible things of the world - a mountain, a river, the sea, fire, storms - rather than abstract concepts. Metaphysical ideas like war or love are only tangentially associated with certain deities, but never their true dominion.

---

## Nature of the Deities
**The deities are spirits presiding over things but not concepts** (like love, war, hatred), and everything (living or material) has its own unique deity. Each mountain, river, sword, each city, town, species of tree, and type of animal, is the domain of a distinct spirit. The most powerful spirits, those presiding over the most ubiquitous of things that pertain to all people, are revered as gods and are worshipped by the masses who vie for their favour and blessings.

**Aethos' Gods** are those most powerful spirits who's domains are so ubiquitous that their masters have become nearly omnipresent. Yet they are also the most distant of all; their consciousnesses are spread so thin that they cannot ever focus their attention in one place. Thus, smaller, local **spirits** tend to be the ones to manifest their wrath or a blessing at the actions of a mortal.

## History 
Before the [[Severance]], hundreds of gods, deities, and god-like beings existed on the world of [[Etheris]]. They had physical forms, walked among the people, and guided the progress, or destruction, of the world’s first great civilisations during the [[Age of Enlightenment]]. 

[[The Severance]] saw their abrupt physical disappearance from the world for reasons as yet unknown. Folk have since come to believe in the spirits of the deities carrying on, collectively presiding over all aspects of the physical world.

## Cultural Associations
Most Aethorian cultures recognise the existence of near innumerable spirits, though they typically closely worship but a few to which they feel most strongly connected. This means that many gods are associated with particular regions and cultural groups, though rarely to the exclusion of all others. 

For example, [[Calderon]] is by far the most popularly worshipped god in the duchy of [[Tourelle]], whilst [[Fir]] is preferred in [[Fellhold]], yet amongst the [[dwarvi|duarvi]] of both regions, [[Thuridin]] receives the most prayers. 

## Worship
Most folk (the Orthodox) happily worship many deities whenever and however they see fit (cf. [Hindu practice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism)). They give prayers to and carry out rituals of whichever god or spirit is most appropriate in any given situation. A simple farmer may pray to [[Fayndal]] at the start of the harvest, to [[Sanguis]] when his wife gives birth, and to [[Calderon]] when an invading army marches towards his lands. This religious approach is referred to as **Orthodox** - the standard. 

### Monotheists
Some clerics, paladins, monks, priests (and the like) may choose to dedicate themselves to a single god or spirit to the exclusion of all others, usually because a mystical event has led them to believe they have been chosen as a sort of champion to that deity.

For these dedicated monotheists, in addition to any societal roles their station asks of them (such as [[Osira]]’s priests being the caretakers of graveyards, or [[Calderon]]’s  having military careers), they also bear responsibility for maintaining the integrity and longevity of their deity's religion and institutions. They bolster the influence and prestige of their deity, and ensure traditions, prayers, rituals, and rites are passed on to the next generations.


> [!NOTE] Death Visions of Osira
When people are near death, they are met with a vision of Osira’s silent avatar. Draped in a tattered dress of shadows, her face half a skull, and wielding a great scythe, she severs their soul from their body and shepherds it into the afterlife. Exactly how Osira persists beyond The Severance is a mystery, as is the nature of the afterlife she is the gateway to – anyone who is resurrected is unable to recall memories of their time after death.
